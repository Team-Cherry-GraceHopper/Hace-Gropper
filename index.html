WHO AM I? YOU HAVE 3 GUESSES

    Clues: 0/4

  • - I began my “first career” at IBM in 1964 - fresh out of grad school but ready to help blaze trails in computer engineering. During my 4 years there, I invented DIS - dynamic instruction scheduling. DIS allowed computers to reorganize how they handled commands to maximize efficiency and data flow, and ultimately, led directly to the creation of modern portable computers.
  • - My “second career” had a much different trajectory. I started out with a low-level programming job, and eventually worked my way up to a position at Xerox’s famous Palo Alto Research Center (PARC) in 1973. Unlike my work at IBM, my work on chip design at PARC was notorious right from the start - I even had a tech revolution named after me.
  • - A major milestone in my career was working with Mead on our Intro to VLSI Design textbook, which promptly took the nation's universities by storm, selling over 70,000 copies and nicknamed "the chip designer's bible"
  • - In 2013,I lobbied a board of directors of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (the largest engineering professional society) for transgender inclusion in the IEEE’s Code of Ethics.
